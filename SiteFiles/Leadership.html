Ji Hoon (Andy) Kim
Vice President

Hello, my name is Andy Kim and I am the Vice President of Tau Beta Pi at Colorado Beta. Currently, I am an undergrad in ECE/Applied Mathematics and expecting to graduate in 2017. I chose electrical engineering out of sheer love for mathematics, creating things, and hopes for impacting my profession. I carry these passions to our own organization, Tau Beta Pi, as well in order to help create a change.

A little about me, I grew up mostly in Boulder having moved here at a young age. Coming from a family of electrical engineers, I could not avoid the influence of mathematics and science early on. Unsurprisingly, combined with the influence of mathematics early on, deciding to go into engineering was not a difficult nor indecisive decision.

I also have many pastimes to accompany my love for engineering. My hobbies include rock climbing, playing tennis, practicing the piano and hanging out with friends. In addition, I enjoy messing around with circuits, programming and simulating things as well.

I look forward to an amazing year with Tau Beta Pi!

Dr. Keith Porter
Faculty Advisor

Dr. Porter is a Research Professor in Structural Engineering and Structural Mechanics at the University of Colorado Boulder and faculty advisor to the university chapters of the American Society of Civil Engineers and Tau Beta Pi. He specializes in societal risk from natural disasters, seismic vulnerability of buildings, and 2nd generation performance-based earthquake engineering (PBEE-2). Notable works include the Mitigation Saves 4:1 benefit-cost ratio study, FEMA P-58 guidelines for PBEE-2, USGS’s ShakeOut, ARkStorm, Tsunami Scenario, and HayWired planning scenarios, the Global Earthquake Model’s vulnerability methodologies, and the Community Action Plan for Seismic Safety study that informed San Francisco’s mandatory soft-story retrofit ordinance. He is a licensed Professional Engineer and author of 140 scholarly and professional works.
